        body:
          - "1064MFG LLC is a micro-scale design and prototyping studio which was created to formalize several projects I've been working on."
          - "What sets 1064MFG apart is a commitment to working at the individual level, and curating projects to make sure there is mutual alignment in our mission."
      - heading: "What does the name stand for?"
        body:
          - "The name is inspired by the 1064nm laser diode, which is commonly used for fiber laser engraving!"
      - heading: "What's the deal with the tree?"
        body:
          - "The 'Lone Evergreen' is my maker mark. It is how I sign my work."
          - "It symbolizes a dedication of forging your own path, building solutions to stand the test of time, and also represents my connection with the Pacific Northwest." 
      - heading: "Our Design Language"
        body:
          - "I love bold designs that tell a story. As someone who is colorblind, I prefer to build bold colors and designs atop one another, like layers of metal being forged together. I go the extra mile for practical effects, often giving props multiple topcoats to create texture, and natural wear patterns - and draw inspiration from retro cassette futurism themes from franchises like Star Wars and Alien."
      - heading: "What is our Mission Statement?"
        quote: true
        body:
          - "Building cool shit for cool people."

  - code: "OP-02"

    image: "/img/about-zack.jpg"

    body:
      - "Hi folks, I'm Zack!"
      - "My creative journey began with basic small business website design, and leveled up in a big way in 2014 with my first 3D printer. Back in those days, you had to assemble your own printer if you wanted a good one - and much like building a computer it's a great way to really learn the ins and outs of the process."
      - "This led to me joining the cosplay community here in Seattle. Over the past decade I've been able to challenge myself with new technologies, materials, and skills that I'd never have otherwise had an opportunity to develop. While it's a super nerdy hobby, it's a humbling experience as well - you're always meeting people who have solved the other pieces of the puzzle you've been working on... and the free exchange of ideas is probably my favorite part about that community. It's like open source software, but for playing dress up!"
      - "At this point I've got thousands of hours behind a 3D printer - building them, maintaining them, running them - and I've worked across basic, engineering, and exotic filaments for everything from cosplay armor to industrial-grade solutions. This skillset has expanded to modeling, graphic design, and most recently laser engraving. All different workflows with their own set of challenges and areas to master!"
      - "Outside of the studio, I maintain a position as a Staff DevOps Engineer at a Nevada-based technology company. Tech has been, and continues to be, a foundational part of my personal and professional life and I don't plan on slowing down."
      - "Thanks for visiting! -Zack"
